The Basics

Essentials of a Good Prompt

Five building blocks that turn an average prompt into a professional, reliable one.

Essential 01
🎯 Clarity
Be specific in your request. The more precise your instruction, the more targeted the output.
❌ "Write me a report." → ✅ "Draft a 500-word compliance report for senior management with: background, findings, risks, and recommendations."
Essential 02
🌍 Context
Provide background, assumptions, and environment so AI can tailor its response appropriately.
"Assume Indian corporate regulations, professional communication standards, and a Big4 consulting context."
Essential 03
🔒 Constraints
Add limits — word count, tone, format — to keep outputs focused and usable.
"Keep it under 200 words, bullet points only, formal tone, no jargon."
Essential 04
👤 Role Assignment
Tell AI who it should be. Assigning a role dramatically improves response quality and relevance.
"You are a Senior Fraud Investigator with 20 years of experience. Draft a case summary for..."
Essential 05
🔄 Iteration
Refine by asking follow-ups — just like you would with a colleague. Great outputs rarely come from a single prompt.
"Make it more concise." / "Add a risk section." / "Rewrite in a more formal tone."
Techniques

Prompting Techniques

Advanced methods used by professionals to consistently get reliable, high-quality AI outputs.

🎛️
Master Prompt
A single, reusable prompt that captures your style, rules, and preferences — your personal AI operating system. Set it once, use it every session.
💡 "You are my productivity co-pilot. Always respond with clarity and professionalism. Use Indian business context. Ask clarifying questions if my request is vague."
🧾
System Prompt
Instructions that guide AI behaviour behind the scenes. Use when you want consistency and guardrails across an entire session.
💡 "Always act as an Ethics & Compliance advisor. Never fabricate data. Prioritize accuracy over creativity."
📚
Few-Shot Prompting
Provide 2–3 examples of Q&A before your real question. This trains the model on-the-fly to match your expected format and style.
💡 Give AI examples of the output you want, then ask it to produce the real one in the same pattern.
🧠
Chain-of-Thought
Ask AI to explain its reasoning step by step before giving the final answer. Especially useful for audits, investigations, and logic-heavy tasks.
💡 "Think through this step by step before giving your conclusion."
🎯
Resetting & Refining
When AI drifts or gets "too trained" on past context, use a soft reset (remove a specific instruction) or hard reset (start completely fresh).
💡 Soft: "Ignore my earlier instruction about tone." Hard: "Reset all context. Respond as if I'm a completely new user."
Advanced Hacks

Power User Hacks

Two advanced techniques that take prompting to a professional level.

01
🧑‍💻
Prompt Engineer Mode
Instead of asking AI for answers, make it act like a Prompt Engineer. It asks clarifying questions and rewrites your rough query into a sharper, more powerful prompt. You then copy-paste that refined prompt into another AI model for more accurate, reliable outputs.
  • Start a new chat with AI
  • Paste the special "Prompt Engineer Mode" prompt from the library below
  • Share your rough idea or question
  • Get a refined, ready-to-use master prompt back
  • Use that prompt in your main AI session
✅ Why it works: Reduces vagueness, avoids hallucinations, and ensures outputs align with your real goals.
02
🔁
Two-Model Review Loop
Use two AI models in a peer-review loop for extra assurance. Model A generates the response. Model B reviews it for accuracy, clarity, and completeness. Optionally, send the revised output back to Model A for final refinement.
  • Copy your question into Model A (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.)
  • Take the answer and feed it into Model B with: "Review this for errors, missing details, and clarity"
  • Compare both versions — choose or merge the best
  • For critical documents, send back to Model A for final polish
✅ Why it works: Adds a peer-review layer, reduces bias, and is ideal for compliance drafts and professional documents.

Best Practices

Guidelines for Better Prompting

  • 🚀
    Start with action verbs: summarise, draft, analyse, compare, extract, rewrite.
  • 🎯
    State audience or purpose: "for senior leadership", "for a LinkedIn post", "for an automation workflow".
  • 🧩
    Break down multi-step tasks into structured parts rather than one massive prompt.
  • 🔁
    Test, refine, and reuse prompts that work well — build your personal library over time.
  • 📌
    Use XML or JSON structures when you need consistently formatted outputs like tables or reports.
