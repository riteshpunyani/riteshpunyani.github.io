The Big Picture

How Data Science & ML Relate

Think of Data Science as the bigger umbrella — ML is one of the most powerful tools inside it.

📊 DATA SCIENCE
Computer Science · Statistics · Domain Knowledge · Coding
🧠 MACHINE LEARNING
Models that learn from data and predict automatically
Data Science asks: "What does the data tell us?"
ML goes further: "Can we use past patterns to predict what happens next?"

Data Science is an interdisciplinary field combining principles from computer science, mathematics, statistics, and domain knowledge to extract hidden patterns, build predictive models, and inform data-driven decisions.

Machine Learning is one of the power tools inside Data Science — the part that allows systems to learn automatically from past data and make predictions about new situations without being explicitly programmed.

Together, they form the backbone of modern AI applications — from the fraud detection systems protecting banks to the recommendation engines powering Netflix and Amazon.

Foundations

The Role of Maths & Statistics

At its core, Machine Learning is applied mathematics and statistics.

📐
Statistics
Helps in understanding patterns, averages, correlations, and probabilities in data — the foundation for understanding what the data is telling you.
In marketing: Statistics identifies which variables (age, gender, past purchases) correlate most with buying behaviour.
🔢
Mathematics
Algebra, calculus, and linear algebra provide the tools to build and optimise predictive models — the engine that turns data into predictions.
In ML: Mathematics builds a model that predicts which new customers are most likely to buy, based on past patterns.

Real-World Use Cases

ML in Action

From boardrooms to hospitals — how ML is delivering measurable impact.

01
💰
Smarter Marketing
Instead of random ads, ML predicts which customers are most likely to purchase — dramatically reducing acquisition costs and boosting revenue. With global corporations spending billions on ads, the cost-saving potential is enormous.
Classification · Propensity
02
🔐
Fraud Detection
Banks use ML to detect unusual transaction patterns in real time — analysing amount, location, device, and frequency simultaneously. ACFE estimates organisations lose 5% of annual revenue to fraud.
Anomaly Detection · Classification
03
🏥
Healthcare Predictions
ML models predict disease risk from health records — blood pressure, age, genetic history — enabling early intervention and significantly better treatment outcomes.
Risk Scoring · Classification
04
📦
Supply Chain Optimisation
Predicts which products will be in demand based on season, location, and sales history — preventing costly overstocking and stockouts while improving working capital.
Time Series · Regression
