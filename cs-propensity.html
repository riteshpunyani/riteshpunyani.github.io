🎯 Business Problem
Marketing was spending broadly to promote Product X across a large customer base, with low conversion and high acquisition cost.

Question: Which customers should we target to maximise conversion while minimising cost?

Objective: Build a propensity model producing a ranked score (0–1) for each customer indicating probability of purchase in the next 30 days — then target the top segments.
🧾 Dataset & Features
Target variable (dependent): purchase_next_30d — 1 if customer purchased in next 30 days, else 0.
Source: CRM + transaction logs + web/mobile interactions (anonymised). Time window: past 12 months.
Independent Variables (Features)
  • Recency — days since last purchase
  • Frequency — purchases in last 90 days
  • Monetary — avg order value / total spend
  • Engagement — page views, email opens
  • Product interaction — cart adds, browse time
  • Demographics — segment, region
  • Promotion exposure — past discount response
Models Tested
  • Logistic Regression — baseline, interpretable
  • Random Forest — higher accuracy
  • XGBoost — best overall performance
  • 80/20 train/test split (time-aware)
  • GridSearch CV for hyperparameter tuning
  • SHAP for feature explainability
✔️ Why These Metrics
ROC-AUC: Good for overall ranking performance — does the model distinguish buyers from non-buyers?

Precision@k / Lift: Directly shows business impact — top 10% of customers convert at X times the baseline rate.

Calibration: Ensures predicted probabilities reflect real-world conversion rates — important for setting decision thresholds.
