Why It Matters

Why Data Analytics in Compliance?

Fraud and misconduct hide behind large volumes of transactions, emails, and approvals. Analytics makes the invisible visible.

🕵️
Detect Early
Identify early warning signs and unusual behaviours before they escalate into major fraud events or compliance failures.
🔮
Predict & Prevent
Move beyond detection — use predictive models to anticipate where misconduct is likely to occur and intervene proactively.
📈
Scale Coverage
Analyse 100% of transactions instead of relying on manual sampling — dramatically increasing coverage and reducing blind spots.
Framework

Fraud Analytics Framework

Specialised analytical tests and use cases applied across seven domains. Click any domain to expand.

💰
Accounts Payable & Procurement
Ghost vendors · Kickbacks · Duplicate payments
▼
  • Duplicate Payment Test — same amount / date / vendor match
  • Benford's Law — detect abnormal number distributions in invoices
  • Vendor–Employee Link Analysis — shared addresses, phone or bank accounts
  • Price Variance Analysis — flag vendors with consistent overpricing vs peers
  • Round-Number or Month-End Billing Check
  • Three-Way Match Exceptions — invoices without matching PO / receipt
Impact: Prevents financial leakages, exposes collusion networks, strengthens internal controls.
🏦
Banking & AML
Transaction layering · Structuring · Shell entities
▼
  • Transaction Pattern Deviation — accounts exceeding normal activity thresholds
  • Geo-Velocity & IP Analysis — logins or transfers from abnormal locations
  • Structuring / Smurfing Detection — multiple small transactions below reporting limits
  • Entity Link Analysis — detect relationships among shell entities
  • KYC Data Inconsistency Check — mismatched customer identity attributes
  • Rapid In-and-Out Movement — flag accounts with quick fund cycling
Impact: Supports STR/SAR reporting, improves AML model precision, strengthens bank integrity.
🧾
Expense & Reimbursement Fraud
Falsified · Inflated · Personal claims
▼
  • Employee Behaviour Profiling — compare individual claims to department norms
  • Weekend / Off-Day Claims Test — reimbursements during non-working periods
  • Round-Amount Expense Check — spot fabricated or estimated bills
  • Duplicate Invoice Scan — same receipts reused across submissions
  • Split Expense Test — repeated claims just below approval thresholds
Impact: Improves financial discipline and deters policy violations.
🌐
Third-Party & Vendor Integrity
Sanctions · Conflict of interest · Reputational risk
▼
  • Beneficial Ownership Matching — cross-verify vendor ownership data
  • Sanctions & Watchlist Screening — OFAC, UNSC, restricted lists
  • Vendor Network Mapping — common addresses or directors
  • Payment Frequency Analysis — abnormal patterns or new high-risk vendors
  • Geolocation Risk Indexing — score vendors by country risk & transparency
Impact: Strengthens ethical sourcing and protects organisational reputation.
⚖️
Compliance Monitoring
Control failures · Ethics trends · Policy breaches
▼
  • Hotline Data Text Mining — recurring complaint themes and risk zones
  • Investigation Closure Time Trend — SLA breaches and case backlogs
  • Sentiment Analysis on Survey Comments — detect disengaged populations
  • Policy Exception Tracking — quantify rule breaches by function or region
  • Control Effectiveness Metrics — track audit outcomes and residual risk scores
Impact: Enables continuous monitoring and proactive ethical risk management.
👥
HR & Conflict of Interest
Ghost employees · Payroll fraud · COI
▼
  • Employee–Vendor Match Tests — phone numbers, PANs, or addresses
  • Duplicate Bank Accounts — multiple employees sharing the same account
  • Attendance vs Productivity Correlation — outlier analysis
  • High Overtime Analysis — consistently excessive overtime claims
Impact: Identifies conflicts of interest, ghost employees, and payroll fraud.
📈
Sales & Revenue Manipulation
Channel stuffing · Fictitious sales · Revenue recognition
▼
  • End-of-Period Spike Analysis — sales clustering near month-end
  • Credit Note Reversal Tests — frequent sales reversals after revenue recognition
  • Outlier Revenue per Customer — z-score or percentile analysis
  • Returns vs Discounts Ratio — unusually high returns or discounts
Impact: Detects revenue recognition fraud and channel stuffing schemes.
